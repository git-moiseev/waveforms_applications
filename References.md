# References

1. Cordes, J. M., & Lazio, T. J. W. (1991). **Interstellar Scattering Effects on the Detection of Narrow-Band Signals.** *The Astrophysical Journal*, **376**, 123–134.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1086/170261
   PDF: https://articles.adsabs.harvard.edu/pdf/1991ApJ...376..123C
   (Classic paper showing that interstellar spectral broadening is typically below 0.1 Hz at 1 GHz, while scintillation is the dominant propagation effect.) :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

2. Cordes, J. M., Lazio, T. J. W., & Sagan, C. (1997). **Scintillation-Induced Intermittency in SETI.** *The Astrophysical Journal*, **487**, 782–788.
   arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/astro-ph/9707039
   (Demonstrates that interstellar scintillation can both increase and decrease the probability of detecting weak narrowband extraterrestrial signals.)

3. Cordes, J. M., & Lazio, T. J. W. (2002). **NE2001.I: A New Model for the Galactic Distribution of Free Electrons and its Fluctuations.**
   arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/astro-ph/0207156
   (Standard model of the Galactic free-electron distribution used for estimating dispersion and scattering.) :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

4. Cordes, J. M., & Lazio, T. J. W. (2003). **NE2001.II: Using Radio Propagation Data to Construct a Model for the Galactic Distribution of Free Electrons.**
   arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/astro-ph/0301598
   (Describes the observational basis of the NE2001 model and discusses localized scattering regions.) :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

5. Cordes, J. M. (2000). **Interstellar Scattering: Radio Sensing of Deep Space Through the Turbulent Interstellar Medium.**
   In *Radio Astronomy at Long Wavelengths*, Geophysical Monograph Series, Vol. 119.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1029/GM119p0105
   (Comprehensive review of radio-wave propagation through the turbulent interstellar plasma.) :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

6. Brzycki, B., Siemion, A. P. V., de Pater, I., Cordes, J. M., Gajjar, V., Lacki, B., & Sheikh, S. (2023). **On Detecting Interstellar Scintillation in Narrowband Radio SETI.**
   *The Astrophysical Journal*, **952**, 46.
   arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.08793
   (Modern analysis of narrowband SETI observations including the effects of interstellar scintillation.)
